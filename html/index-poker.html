History

"Poker's origins can be traced back to the early 19th century in the United States, where it was played in riverboats and saloons along the Mississippi River. Some historians believe its roots go even further, with influences from Persian card games like As-Nas and European games like Poque and Brag.
During the American Civil War, soldiers played poker to pass the time, popularizing it across different states. Over the decades, poker evolved with the introduction of new variants, betting structures, and strategies. The advent of online poker in the late 1990s brought a new wave of players, making it accessible to anyone with an internet connection. Today, poker is a global phenomenon, featured in high-stakes tournaments, television broadcasts, and online platforms where millions of players compete for glory and financial rewards.
From its humble beginnings as a casual gambling game to its status as a competitive mind sport, poker has stood the test of time as one of the most engaging and strategic card games ever created."
